How Streaming Music Services Work

Most internet music players work in a similar way: the user picks a musician or a song and then the site begins streaming the song's data to your device.

In the following we want to explain you the technology behind popular streaming music services like Spotify.

Technology

Services like Spotify and Soundcloud rely on a peer-to-peer (P2P) network.

When the user selects a song, Spotify's computers immediately begin sending the data to your computer. At the same time, it looks for other Spotify users who have the song on their computers. When it finds the track, it sends bits of the song to your computer.


Tricks

Another trick these streaming music services use is caching (storing) the songs the user listens to right on their hard drive. That means, that their computer plays the song. The size of the cache is set by default to 10 GB, but users can adjust its size.


Spotify is very smart. The site knows that most of its users listen to albums from the beginning to the end; so if you start playing the first song of an album, Spotify begins preloading subsequent tracks, too, so that you never experience playback delays.


Format and Speed

All songs are in the Vorbis format. That means, that this format is compressed to transfer the files more easily.

Most songs stream at 160 kbps (kilobits per second), although Premius users can stream at a higher-quality 320kbps.
